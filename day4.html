After a relaxed breakfast we will head to Tabo Monastery which date backs to 996 CE in the Tibetan year of the Fire Ape.It is is noted for being the oldest continuously operating Buddhist enclave in both India and the Himalayas.

Then we will go ahead to Dhankar village, the former capital of Spiti, it includes Dhankar Monasterywhich is built on a high mountain spur overlooking the confluence of the Spiti and Pin Rivers. It is one of the world’s most spectacular scenery.

Then we will hike to Dhankarlake which is a steep walk of 45 minutes. The Dhankar Lake is sited about 1.5 km from the village at a height of 13,500 ft.However camping is not allowed and the climb is optional.Spend some time at the lake and head backto our hotel and enjoy the chill weather of Kaza or else you can take an experience of Stargazing.
